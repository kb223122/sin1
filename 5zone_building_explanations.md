# Simple Explanations - 5Zone Building Terms

## HVAC CAPACITIES

### **Cooling Capacity (63.71 kW)**
**What it is**: Maximum cooling power your AC system can provide  
**Role**: Removes heat from building when it's hot outside  
**Simple**: Like the "max speed" of your air conditioner

### **Heating Capacity (26.63 kW)**
**What it is**: Maximum heating power your system can provide  
**Role**: Adds heat to building when it's cold outside  
**Simple**: Like the "max heat" your heater can produce

---

## DESIGN LOADS

### **Design Cooling Load (29.71 kW)**
**What it is**: Amount of heat that needs to be removed on the hottest day  
**Role**: Tells you what size AC you actually need  
**Simple**: The "workload" your AC must handle during summer peak

### **Design Heating Load (15.61 kW)**
**What it is**: Amount of heat needed to keep warm on the coldest day  
**Role**: Tells you what size heater you actually need  
**Simple**: The "workload" your heater must handle during winter peak

---

## THERMAL PROPERTIES

### **U-Factor (e.g., 0.384 W/m²-K for walls)**
**What it is**: How easily heat passes through a material  
**Role**: Lower = better insulation (less heat loss)  
**Simple**: Like "heat leak rate" - how fast heat escapes

### **R-Value (e.g., R-14.8 for walls)**
**What it is**: How well a material resists heat flow  
**Role**: Higher = better insulation  
**Simple**: Opposite of U-factor - resistance to heat flow

### **Thermal Mass (~1,200-1,500 kJ/K)**
**What it is**: Building's ability to store heat (like a battery)  
**Role**: Smooths temperature swings - keeps building cooler longer in heat, warmer longer in cold  
**Simple**: Like thermal "inertia" - slow to heat up, slow to cool down

---

## HEAT TRANSFER

### **Heat Loss to Outdoor (~54 kW)**
**What it is**: Heat escaping from building to outside  
**Role**: Determines how much heating energy you need  
**Simple**: Heat "leaking out" through walls, roof, windows when it's cold

### **Heat Gain from Outdoor (~25-36 kW)**
**What it is**: Heat entering building from outside  
**Role**: Determines how much cooling energy you need  
**Simple**: Heat "coming in" from sun, hot air when it's hot outside

### **Latent Cooling (12.82 kW)**
**What it is**: Energy used to remove moisture (humidity) from air  
**Role**: Makes air feel comfortable, not just cool  
**Simple**: "Dehumidification power" - removes sweat/dampness from air

---

## QUICK REFERENCE

| Term | Think of it as... | Why it matters |
|------|-------------------|----------------|
| **Cooling Capacity** | AC's "max power" | Must be bigger than peak cooling load |
| **Heating Capacity** | Heater's "max power" | Must be bigger than peak heating load |
| **Design Loads** | "Peak demands" | Size equipment to handle worst-case scenario |
| **U-Factor** | "Heat leak speed" | Lower = less energy wasted |
| **R-Value** | "Insulation quality" | Higher = less energy needed |
| **Thermal Mass** | "Heat storage battery" | Reduces temperature swings, saves energy |
| **Heat Loss** | "Energy escaping" | Determines heating needs |
| **Heat Gain** | "Energy entering" | Determines cooling needs |
| **Latent Cooling** | "Dehumidifier power" | Makes air feel comfortable, not just cool |

---

## KEY INSIGHTS

1. **Capacity > Load**: Equipment must be sized larger than peak loads (safety margin)
2. **Better insulation (lower U, higher R)**: Less energy needed = lower bills
3. **Thermal mass**: Acts like a buffer, reducing peak loads
4. **Solar gain**: Major cooling driver (25-36 kW includes sun coming through windows)
5. **Floor heat loss**: Biggest weakness (29.4 kW of 54 kW total) - floor poorly insulated
